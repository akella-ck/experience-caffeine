# Experience Caffeine — Product Roadmap

The roadmap grows Experience Caffeine from a high-quality local learning tool into a durable, evidence-aware brewing companion. Each phase should preserve clear user control: recommendations explain their inputs, grinder guidance remains a starting point, and users change one major brew variable at a time.

## Phase 1 — User authentication

Introduce secure accounts, equipment preferences, accessibility settings, and consent-aware product analytics. Keep all educational content usable without signing in.

## Phase 2 — Cloud-synced brew journal

Persist journal entries and saved recipes across devices, add export and account deletion, support offline-first entry capture, and establish a versioned journal schema.

## Phase 3 — Personalized recipe engine

Use equipment, coffee, past brews, and explicit taste feedback to rank transparent starting recipes. Show which observations influenced a recommendation and preserve a manual mode.

## Phase 4 — Coffee bag scanning

Let users capture a bag label to prefill roaster, origin, process, variety, altitude, and roast date. Require confirmation before saving uncertain fields and never invent unreadable details.

## Phase 5 — AI brew coach with verified retrieval

Add a conversational coach grounded in reviewed first-party curriculum, manufacturer documentation, and clearly attributed specialty-coffee sources. Recommendations should expose citations, uncertainty, and safety boundaries.

## Phase 6 — Native mobile app

Build a native experience focused on counter-readable guided brewing, offline recipes, camera capture, tactile timers, and reliable background notifications.

## Phase 7 — Bluetooth scale integration

Connect supported scales for live flow rate, cumulative weight, and pour-stage detection. Provide a complete manual fallback and make device support explicit.

## Phase 8 — Computer-vision grind analysis

Research on-device particle distribution analysis with controlled capture guidance and calibration references. Present estimates as ranges and validate them against measured sieve data before release.

## Phase 9 — Roaster partnerships

Create verified roaster profiles and bag-specific starting recipes while preserving editorial independence. Clearly label sponsored or partner-provided content.

## Phase 10 — Community recipes

Enable versioned recipe sharing, equipment-aware adaptation, tasting notes, moderation, and useful credibility signals. Make it easy to fork a recipe without overwriting the original.

## Cross-phase foundations

- privacy, export, deletion, and secure defaults
- accessible interaction and captioned or transcribed learning content
- content provenance and review dates
- observable recommendation quality and rollback capability
- fast, resilient experiences on low-end mobile devices
- international units, localization, and region-aware coffee terminology

## Corporate platform phases

### 1. Production authentication

Replace demo credentials and client-side guards with server-issued sessions, secure password handling, recovery, rate limiting, and multi-factor authentication for corporate accounts.

### 2. Multi-tenant organizations

Create durable tenant boundaries for cafés, roasters, training organizations, locations, and integrations with enforced data isolation.

### 3. Role and permission management

Add organization owners, roasters, quality managers, café managers, trainers, and baristas with resource- and action-level permissions.

### 4. Real database persistence

Persist versioned recipes, roast profiles, batches, lessons, approvals, troubleshooting records, and audit history through validated server APIs.

### 5. Verified roasting curriculum

Commission and review machine-aware roasting education with named subject-matter experts, provenance, revision dates, and knowledge checks.

### 6. Roast curve import

Import documented curve formats as read-only records, map events explicitly, preserve the source file, and surface sensor limitations.

### 7. Read-only machine telemetry

Connect approved adapters for observational telemetry with connection health, stale-data detection, local gateway security, and complete manual fallbacks.

### 8. Controlled machine integrations

Consider control only after vendor approval, machine-specific validation, human confirmation, fail-safe behavior, audit logging, and independent safety review.

### 9. Inventory and green-coffee management

Track lots, intake measurements, storage, allocations, production usage, and traceability without replacing regulated inventory or accounting systems prematurely.

### 10. Multi-location recipe synchronization

Publish approved, versioned recipes to assigned locations while preserving local equipment context, acknowledgement, and rollback.

### 11. Staff certification

Add assignments, assessments, observed checklists, expiration, retraining, and manager verification.

### 12. Cupping and quality analytics

Connect cupping, roast deviation, recipe adherence, corrective action, and location-comparison workflows using meaningful, reviewable metrics.

### 13. Mobile companion app

Support counter and production-floor workflows with offline capture, background timers, notifications, barcode scanning, and secure device enrollment.

### 14. Smart-scale and espresso-machine integrations

Add documented read-only measurements first, make compatibility explicit, and preserve manual entry for every critical workflow.
